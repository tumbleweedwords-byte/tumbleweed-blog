A Berlin U-Bahn in winter, there’s a punk woman on the platform chewing gum the colour of her hair. A toothless man watches her move. The Clash plays in a young man’s ears because some sounds were born to travel. This is what lands in your inbox every week. Not a newsletter about writing, but stories found on the road.

A story that explores brotherhood and the human condition

In growing pains, two boys sit cross-legged on worn carpet eating Chicken Kiev off steaming plates while their dads drink away their wages at the pub. Controls the shape of samosas. Chewing gum that matches the colour of her hair. That level of specific.

“You’re not a serious person anymore, Gabriel. Was I ever? Before the drink, yes. Well, sort of.”

A box room in Whitechapel. Love Is A Dog From Hell on the floor. A flip phone and a rolled cigarette. He pours himself another measure, attempts to call her back. Everything and nothing happening all at once, people moving through the world at good speed.
