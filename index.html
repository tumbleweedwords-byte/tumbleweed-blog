From the archive · Berlin, winter

Bitesize fiction and poetry, directly in your inbox

“The carriages are full of inviting people I never talk to. Everyone wears black so hard you don’t notice after a while that there are differing shades. A punk appears on the platform, chewing gum that matches the colour of her hair.”
David — ‘train in vain’ — Tumbleweed Words

A Berlin U-Bahn in winter, there’s a punk woman on the platform chewing gum the colour of her hair. A toothless man watches her move. The Clash plays in a young man’s ears because some sounds were born to travel. This is what lands in your inbox every week. Not a newsletter about writing, but stories found on the road.

On the writing

Flash fiction and prose poetry delivered in bitesize portions to your inbox every week. The stories contain cities as characters, working class voices ripped from the asphalt, love affairs from overseas.

Where nomadic words are found

Somewhere out there, lost in Seoul. On a night train between Warsaw and Berlin. Sat on a park bench in Buenos Aires at four in the morning. Stories are found on the road, written in the moment, sent from anywhere, out there.
