About Tumbleweed Words

Internationally published fiction writer and poet. Pushcart-nominated. Writing from trains, borrowed rooms, and strange cities across Europe and South America.

Read the newsletter → Explore Stories & Craft

This is the writing and craft side of Tumbleweed Words — essays on minimalist fiction, flash fiction technique, literary publishing, and what it actually looks like to maintain a writing practice on the road. The fiction and poetry lives on the newsletter. The thinking about the work lives here.

Read internationally published fiction and poetry

My fiction and poetry has been published internationally in New York, Lisbon, Toronto, and London — in Adelaide Magazine, Litro Magazine, Cleaver Magazine, The Dundee Anthology, Argentina Independent, and The View From Here. I have been nominated for the Pushcart Prize. Most of what I write is produced in transit — on trains, in hotel rooms, at café tables in cities I am passing through. The writing draws on working-class experience, displacement, memory, and the small moments that hold the weight of a life.

Tumbleweed Words has been running on Substack for five years with over a thousand subscribers. The best place to start is the Best Of collection, or dive into the full archive.

Explore craft essays on flash fiction and minimalism

The craft essays on this site cover what I actually know: how to write flash fiction, minimalist fiction techniques, writing while nomadic, and where to submit your work. Written for serious readers and writers of literary fiction — not beginners looking for generic tips. Each essay draws on five years of independent publishing and a practice built on the road.

Subscribe to the newsletter or get in touch

The best way to reach me is through Substack, where I read all replies to the newsletter. Every email gets read. If you write literary fiction, flash fiction, or poetry and want to connect, reply to any edition — that is where the conversation happens.
