Flash Fiction Set in Buenos Aires

Minimalist flash fiction set in Buenos Aires, Argentina — short stories written from the ground, in the streets, with tango in the streets, bookshops that never close, the humidity of summer.

There is something that happens to writing when it is produced inside a city rather than about it from a distance. The sentences change. They pick up the rhythm of the streets, the specific weight of the air, the quality of light at a particular hour. Buenos Aires does something specific to prose.

The flash fiction in Tumbleweed Words that was written in or about Buenos Aires shares certain qualities — tango in the streets, bookshops that never close, the humidity of summer. These are not travel writing pieces. They are not postcards. They are short fictions that use Buenos Aires the way Hemingway used Paris: as a pressure, not a backdrop.

What place does to minimalist fiction

The discipline of flash fiction — complete stories under 1,000 words — forces a writer to choose what a place actually means to them. You cannot describe everything. You choose one detail, one moment, one sensory impression, and let it carry the weight of the whole city. In Buenos Aires, that detail is almost always something unexpected: not the famous landmark, but the thing you notice at 6am or 11pm when the tourist version has gone to sleep.

The best flash fiction set in a place doesn't describe the place. It becomes the place — it makes the reader feel the particular quality of being there, without explaining what that quality is.

The nomadic writing practice

Writing from cities you are passing through rather than living in produces a particular kind of attention. Everything is still strange. The strangeness is the material. Most of the flash fiction published in Tumbleweed Words was written in transit — in the first week in a new city, before the strangeness fades into familiarity and the prose goes to sleep.

Buenos Aires is one of those cities that keeps you awake. Read more about the nomadic writing practice that produces this kind of work, or read the guide to writing flash fiction that captures a specific place in under 1,000 words.
